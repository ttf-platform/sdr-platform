# How AI writes personalized emails

Every email Mirvo drafts is generated fresh for that specific prospect. This article explains what inputs go into each draft and what you can do to improve them.

## What's different from templates

Most outbound tools use templates with merge fields: "Hi `{firstName}`, I noticed `{company}` recently..." That pattern is immediately recognizable to buyers. It reads like a form letter because it is one.

Mirvo doesn't use templates. It reads the prospect's actual context and your ICP, then writes an email from scratch for that person.

The difference in a draft: instead of "Hi Sarah, I noticed Acme Corp recently...", you get an email that references Sarah's specific role, the company's recent hiring pattern or press coverage, and frames the pain in terms that match what a VP at a Series B SaaS company actually cares about.

## What goes into each draft

Three inputs combine to produce a draft:

**1. Prospect data**
- Company name, size, industry, location
- Prospect's role and title
- Recent company activity (funding, hiring, news, if available)
- Any extra columns you imported (custom fields, notes)

**2. Your Master ICP**
- Who you sell to (narrows relevance)
- What pain you solve (shapes the angle)
- Your tone of voice (shapes the phrasing)

**3. Signal context (if active)**
- If the draft was triggered by a Signal, the matched event is included: what happened, when, and why it was flagged

The AI combines these three inputs and writes a draft that makes the case for reaching out to this specific person, at this specific moment.

## What makes a good draft

Good drafts share four qualities:

- **Specific first sentence.** Not a compliment or a generic opener. Something concrete about the prospect's situation.
- **Relevant pain.** The problem Mirvo names should actually be a problem for this person, in their role, at their stage.
- **Soft CTA.** No "Can I book 15 minutes?" in the first email. A softer ask ("Worth a quick look?") gets more replies.
- **Short.** Under 120 words for the first email. Founders read mobile.

## When drafts are generic

If your drafts feel generic, start with the Master ICP. The most common cause is an ICP that's too broad or written in marketing language rather than buyer language.

Second: check whether the prospects have enough context. Prospects imported with only email + first name + company get narrower personalization than prospects with role, industry, and custom notes.

<Callout type="tip">
Add a "notes" column to your CSV with one line about why each prospect is in your list. The AI uses it.
</Callout>

## Vendor visibility

The AI writing your emails is part of Mirvo's platform. Recipient inboxes show Mirvo's sender domain and your name in the From header. Nothing in the email reveals how it was written.

# How Mirvo works

Most outbound tools force you to do all the work: build a list, write templates, manage deliverability, sift through replies. Mirvo handles the work and gives you back the decisions.

You pick the prospects (yours or Mirvo's built-in search). AI drafts personalized emails. You approve. Mirvo sends and sorts the replies. Optionally, layer Signals to fire drafts only on buying moments.

This page is the 3-minute orientation. Detailed walkthroughs link from the bottom.

## The flow

<Steps>
1. **Choose your prospects.** Import your contacts (CSV or CRM export), or search Mirvo's prospect database (companies and decision-makers across most B2B verticals). Either way, you control who's in scope.
2. **AI drafts personalized emails.** Each prospect gets an email written from their context and your Master ICP. Not templated. Not "Hi `{firstName}`, I noticed your company..."
3. **Optional: layer Signals for higher precision.** Signals are custom intent detectors. Examples: a target hires an SDR, raises a Series A, posts about a specific problem, or launches a competitor product. Add a signal to a campaign and Mirvo queues drafts only when that signal fires. Without signals, drafts go out for every chosen prospect.
4. **Approve and send.** Drafts land in your Approval Queue. You read, edit, approve. Mirvo sends through managed deliverability. Replies land in your Mirvo inbox, sorted by intent.
</Steps>

## What's different from traditional cold email

| Traditional outbound | Mirvo |
|---|---|
| You scrape or buy a prospect list | Import yours or use Mirvo's built-in prospect search |
| You write email templates | AI drafts personalized emails per prospect |
| Timing is guesswork | Optional Signals fire drafts on buying moments |
| You manage domains and warmup | Infrastructure handled, your real inbox stays clean |
| You sift through replies | Replies sorted by intent (positive, objection, out of office, unsubscribe) |

The shift: from list-building chore to "search and go," and from spray-and-pray to optional precision timing. Lower volume, higher reply rate.

## What your first hour looks like

<Callout type="info">
No warmup wait. Your first email can go out within an hour of signing up. Mirvo's deliverability infrastructure handles reputation from day one.
</Callout>

1. **Link a mailbox** (Gmail or Outlook OAuth, 30 seconds)
2. **Fill in your Master ICP** (10 minutes)
3. **Pick your prospects.** Import a list, or search Mirvo's database (5 minutes)
4. **Launch your first campaign.** Optionally add a Signal for buying-moment targeting (5 minutes)
5. **First drafts in the Approval Queue** (within 1 hour, often faster)

Total active time around 20 minutes.

## What Mirvo doesn't do (yet)

To set expectations:

- **Not a CRM.** Mirvo focuses on outbound discovery and sending. Move replies to your CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive, Attio, etc.) via export.
- **Not a meeting scheduler.** Add a Calendly link in your signature.
- **Not LinkedIn outreach.** Email-only for V1.
- **Not a phone dialer.** Email-only.

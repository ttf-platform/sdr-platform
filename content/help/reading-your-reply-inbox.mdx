# Reading your reply inbox

When a prospect replies to a Mirvo-sent email, the reply surfaces in your Mirvo inbox, not just your regular Gmail or Outlook. This article explains what the inbox shows and how to use it.

## The Mirvo inbox

Go to **Inbox** in the sidebar. All replies from Mirvo-sent campaigns appear here, sorted by intent category.

<Screenshot placeholder alt='Mirvo inbox with sorted replies showing intent category badges in different colors' caption='Mirvo inbox with sorted replies showing intent category badges in different colors' />

Replies also land in your regular mailbox. The Mirvo inbox is a layer on top, not a replacement.

## Intent categories

Mirvo classifies each reply into one of five categories:

| Category | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Interested** | Prospect wants to learn more or asked a qualifying question |
| **Not now** | Soft no: timing, not fit. Worth a follow-up in 2-3 months |
| **Not a fit** | Hard no: wrong role, wrong company, or explicit disinterest |
| **Unsubscribe** | Asked to be removed. Mirvo stops all sequences for this prospect automatically |
| **OOO** | Out of office auto-reply. Sequence is paused until they're back |

<Callout type="info">
Unsubscribes are processed immediately. When a reply is categorized as Unsubscribe, Mirvo cancels all pending drafts for that prospect and adds them to a suppression list. They won't appear in future campaigns.
</Callout>

## Recategorizing a reply

If the AI miscategorized a reply, click the category label on the reply and select the correct one. Manual overrides apply immediately and improve future categorization accuracy.

This is worth doing for Interested and Not a fit misclassifications. Getting these right affects how you prioritize follow-up.

## Reading a reply thread

Click any reply to expand the thread. You see:

- The full reply text
- The email that triggered the reply (the Mirvo-sent email in the thread)
- Prospect details: name, company, role, campaign

## What to do with an Interested reply

An Interested reply means the prospect engaged. Next steps depend on your sales process, but a typical pattern:

1. Click through to your regular inbox (or reply directly from the Mirvo inbox)
2. Reply within a few hours. Speed matters here
3. Don't use a template. Write a personal reply that acknowledges what they said

Mirvo doesn't automate responses to interested replies. That's the handoff point from automated outbound to a human conversation.

## Filtering and search

Filter the inbox by:
- Intent category (show only Interested)
- Campaign
- Date range

Use the search to find a specific prospect by name or company.

## Sequence behavior after reply

When a prospect replies (any reply), Mirvo automatically pauses the sequence for that prospect. No follow-up emails go out while a reply thread is open.

If you want to resume the sequence (e.g., the prospect replied "not now, check back later"), you can manually resume the sequence from the prospect's profile in the campaign.

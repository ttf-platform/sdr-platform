# Linking your mailbox

Mirvo sends approved emails through your own mailbox. This keeps the From address as you, not a shared sending pool. This article covers connecting and maintaining your mailbox connection.

## What "linking" means

Mirvo connects to your mailbox via OAuth, the same standard used by calendar apps and productivity tools. You authorize Mirvo to send on your behalf. No password is shared.

When a draft is approved, Mirvo uses this authorization to dispatch the email from your address.

## Supported providers

- **Gmail** (personal and Google Workspace)
- **Outlook** (personal and Microsoft 365)

Other providers are not supported.

## Connecting your mailbox

<Steps>
1. Go to **Settings, Mailboxes**
2. Click **Add mailbox**
3. Choose Gmail or Outlook
4. Complete the OAuth consent screen in the popup
5. Grant all requested permissions (see below)
6. The popup redirects back to Mirvo. The mailbox appears as "Active"
</Steps>

## Permissions requested

Mirvo requests the minimum permissions needed to send email:

| Permission | Why |
|---|---|
| Send email on your behalf | Dispatches approved drafts from your address |
| Read email metadata | Detects replies to Mirvo-sent emails |
| Manage labels (Gmail) | Marks Mirvo-sent threads for reply tracking |

Mirvo does not request access to read your inbox contents, contacts, or calendar.

<Callout type="info">
If your organization's IT policy restricts third-party app access, you may need IT to allowlist Mirvo before the OAuth flow completes. See the troubleshooting section below.
</Callout>

## Adding multiple mailboxes

You can connect more than one mailbox. Go to Settings, Mailboxes, Add mailbox. Each campaign selects which mailbox sends its emails.

Use cases for multiple mailboxes:
- Different segments get email from different team members
- You're running campaigns in parallel and want to distribute send volume

## If your mailbox disconnects

Mailboxes disconnect when you change your account password, revoke app access in Google/Microsoft settings, or when the OAuth token expires (typically after 6-12 months of inactivity).

To reconnect:

<Steps>
1. Go to **Settings, Mailboxes**
2. Click the disconnected mailbox (shows "Disconnected" or "Reconnect required")
3. Click **Reconnect** and complete the OAuth flow again
</Steps>

Campaigns pause automatically when their mailbox disconnects. They resume as soon as the mailbox is reconnected.

## Troubleshooting

**OAuth popup closes without completing:**
- Don't close the popup until it redirects back to Mirvo
- Disable popup blockers for mirvo.ai temporarily

**Gmail: "This app hasn't been verified" warning:**
- Click "Advanced" then "Go to Mirvo (unsafe)" on the Google warning screen. This warning appears for OAuth apps that haven't gone through Google's verification program and is a UX issue, not a security risk.

**Gmail: Google Workspace admin has blocked third-party app access:**
- Ask your admin to allowlist Mirvo's OAuth app, or use a personal Google account for testing

**Outlook: OAuth fails silently:**
- Often caused by conditional access policies requiring MFA in a specific flow. Use a mailbox not behind strict conditional access, or ask IT to add an exception.

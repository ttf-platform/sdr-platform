# Adding a sending domain

By default, Mirvo sends from a shared sending domain with managed reputation. That works from day one and requires zero setup.

For better long-term deliverability and brand consistency, connect a custom domain you own. Emails sent from your own domain build your reputation over time, and recipients see your brand in the From header.

## The 3-step wizard

Go to **Settings, Sending Domains, Add domain**. The wizard guides you through three steps.

### Step 1. Enter your domain

Type the domain you want to send from (e.g., `outreach.yourdomain.com`). Using a subdomain (not your root domain) is recommended: it isolates outbound reputation from your main domain.

<Screenshot src='/help/screenshots/adding-a-sending-domain-1.png' alt='Step 1 of the wizard, domain input field, with annotation showing subdomain recommendation' caption='Step 1 of the wizard, domain input field, with annotation showing subdomain recommendation' />

### Step 2. Add DNS records

Mirvo generates three DNS records: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. Copy them to your DNS provider.

| Record type | What it does |
|---|---|
| SPF (TXT) | Authorizes Mirvo's servers to send on behalf of your domain |
| DKIM (CNAME or TXT) | Cryptographic signature proving emails weren't tampered with |
| DMARC (TXT) | Policy telling receiving servers what to do with failures |

**Cloudflare:** DNS, Add record. Select the type (TXT or CNAME), paste name and value.

**GoDaddy:** DNS Management, Add. Select type, paste fields.

**Namecheap:** Advanced DNS, Add New Record. Select type, paste.

**Route 53:** Hosted Zones, your domain, Create record. Select type, paste.

<Callout type="warning">
DNS propagation takes up to 48 hours, though usually under 2 hours. Mirvo checks periodically and will notify you once verification passes.
</Callout>

<Screenshot placeholder alt='DNS records table in wizard with copy buttons, showing SPF/DKIM/DMARC rows' caption='DNS records table in wizard with copy buttons, showing SPF/DKIM/DMARC rows' />

### Step 3. Verify

Click **Check DNS**. Mirvo queries your DNS records. When all three are detected, your domain is verified and active.

<Screenshot placeholder alt='Step 3 verification screen showing green checkmarks on all three records' caption='Step 3 verification screen showing green checkmarks on all three records' />

## Troubleshooting

**"SPF record not found":** Make sure you added the record as type TXT (not A or CNAME). If you already have an SPF record for the domain, merge the Mirvo include into your existing record rather than adding a second one.

**"DKIM not detected":** Some DNS providers add a trailing dot to CNAMEs automatically. If verification fails, try adding or removing the trailing dot from the record name.

**"DMARC missing":** Add the DMARC record as-is. If you have an existing DMARC record for your root domain, the subdomain inherits it, so you may not need a separate one.

**Records look correct but still failing:** Wait 30 minutes and retry. DNS propagation sometimes takes longer through certain providers.

## After verification

Your domain appears as "Active" in Settings, Sending Domains. New campaigns use it automatically. You can set a different domain per campaign if you have multiple.

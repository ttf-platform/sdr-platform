# Choosing your sending setup

Mirvo gives you three ways to send your campaigns. You pick one when you start, and you can change or add to it later from **Settings → Sending domains**. This article explains what each option does, what it costs you in setup time, and which one fits your situation.

There is no single "best" option. The right choice depends on whether you want to start sending today, how much volume you need, and whether you want a dedicated domain that protects your main address.

## The short version

- **Use your own mailbox** — fastest start, send today, lower daily volume. Best for trying Mirvo and for low-volume outreach.
- **A dedicated domain (managed by Mirvo)** — protects your main domain, scales to full volume, but needs a few weeks of warmup before it runs at full speed.
- **A pre-warmed domain** — skips most of the wait, but the domain isn't uniquely yours. Best when you need volume quickly and accept the trade-off.

## Option 1 — Use your own mailbox

You connect your existing Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 mailbox through a secure sign-in. It takes about 30 seconds.

Because your mailbox is already in daily use, it already has sending reputation. That means **you can start sending the same day** — there's no warmup to wait through. Replies land in your real inbox, and prospects see your real address.

The trade-off is volume. To keep your main address safe, Mirvo caps daily sending on a connected personal mailbox to a low number. That's plenty for testing Mirvo and for steady, low-volume outreach, but it's not built for high-volume campaigns.

<Callout type="tip">
This is the recommended way to start. You get a real result on day one without risking your main address, and you can move to a dedicated domain later if you need more volume.
</Callout>

**Best for:** trying Mirvo, low-volume outreach, anyone who wants to send today.

## Option 2 — A dedicated domain, managed by Mirvo

Mirvo registers a separate sending domain for you (a variation on your brand, for example `getyourcompany.com`), configures the technical records, and sets up the mailboxes. You don't touch any DNS — we handle it.

A dedicated domain keeps your cold outreach completely separate from your main domain, so your primary address is never exposed to cold sending. It's the durable setup for running real volume.

The honest part: a brand-new domain has no reputation yet. Mailbox providers distrust new domains that suddenly send a lot, so the domain has to be warmed up gradually. **Plan on around four weeks** before it runs at full volume — sending starts low and ramps up as reputation builds. During that ramp, your connected personal mailbox (Option 1) carries your real outreach as a bridge, so you're never stuck waiting with nothing going out.

<Callout type="info">
Warmup isn't a switch that flips. The domain takes on real sending volume gradually as it earns reputation — low in the first weeks, full capacity by around week four. This gradual ramp is what keeps you out of spam folders.
</Callout>

**Best for:** anyone running real, ongoing volume who wants a sending domain that's fully theirs and protects their main address.

## Option 3 — A pre-warmed domain

If you need volume quickly and don't want to wait for a fresh domain to warm up, Mirvo can set you up on a domain that's already been warmed.

This skips most of the wait. The trade-off is ownership and reliability: the domain isn't uniquely yours, and pre-warmed domains generally deliver less reliably than a dedicated domain you've warmed yourself, because their reputation is shared. We surface this option honestly — it's a way to move fast, not the most durable setup.

**Best for:** people who need to send volume soon and accept that a shared, pre-warmed domain is a speed-over-durability choice.

## Side by side

| | Your own mailbox | Dedicated domain | Pre-warmed domain |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Setup time** | Minutes | ~4 weeks to full volume | Fast |
| **Send on day one** | Yes | Bridged by your mailbox | Yes |
| **Daily volume** | Low (capped) | Full | Full |
| **Domain is yours** | Your real one | Yes, dedicated | No, shared |
| **Protects main domain** | — | Yes | Yes |
| **Best for** | Trying Mirvo, low volume | Ongoing real volume | Volume in a hurry |

## How to choose

- **Just getting started, or sending in low volume?** Connect your own mailbox. You'll be sending today.
- **Ready to run real, ongoing campaigns?** Order a dedicated domain. Use your connected mailbox as a bridge while it warms up over the first few weeks.
- **Need volume fast and can't wait on warmup?** Ask about a pre-warmed domain, knowing it's a speed-for-durability trade.

You're not locked in. You can start on your own mailbox today and add a dedicated domain whenever you're ready — Mirvo bridges the two so your outreach never stops.

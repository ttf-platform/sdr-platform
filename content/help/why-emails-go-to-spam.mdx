# Why emails go to spam

Cold email deliverability is often mystified. This article covers what actually determines placement, in order of impact.

## The factors, ranked

### 1. Domain reputation (highest impact)

Your sending domain's reputation is the primary signal spam filters use. A new domain with no history sends worse than an established one.

Mirvo manages domain warmup and reputation automatically. You don't need to worry about this unless you're using a custom domain you bring yourself. If you are: see [Adding a sending domain](/help/adding-a-sending-domain).

### 2. Authentication records

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC are DNS records that prove you're authorized to send from your domain. Missing or broken records are an immediate red flag for receiving mail servers.

Check: Settings, Sending Domain, Verify records. All three should show green.

### 3. Content signals

Spam filters read email content. High-signal content issues:

- Spam trigger words in the subject line or body (`free`, `guaranteed`, `limited time offer`)
- High link density: more than 2 links in a short email
- HTML-heavy formatting or large images
- Attachments in cold email (almost always filtered)

Plain text or minimal HTML consistently outperforms designed emails for cold outbound. Mirvo's templates use minimal formatting by design.

### 4. Sending volume and patterns

Sending 500 emails in one hour from a new domain triggers volume-based filters. Mirvo paces sends automatically within your plan's monthly limit.

Don't try to blast a large list all at once by approving hundreds of drafts simultaneously. Approve in batches of 20-50 and let the send queue process them.

### 5. Engagement signals

Spam filters are influenced by what recipients do with your emails: opens, replies, and marked-as-spam. High spam complaints (more than 0.1%) damage domain reputation.

You can't control what any individual does, but you can reduce complaint risk:

- Only send to real, opted-in or genuinely targeted prospects (not scraped lists)
- Keep lists clean: remove bounces immediately

### 6. Recipient server policies

Some corporate email servers are aggressive regardless of your reputation. Financial services, healthcare, and government IT environments often have strict policies that filter cold email by default.

This is not fully controllable. If a specific domain consistently goes to spam for legitimate-looking emails, it may be their server policy.

## What Mirvo handles automatically

- Domain warmup and reputation management
- Send pacing within your plan limits
- Bounce processing and list hygiene
- DKIM signing on Mirvo's sending infrastructure

## What you control

- Authentication records if using a custom domain
- Email content (subject line, body, links, formatting)
- List quality (role accuracy, active prospects)
- Approval cadence (don't batch-approve hundreds at once)

<Callout type="info">
If your emails are landing in spam at a higher-than-normal rate, check in this order: authentication records, subject line, link density, list quality. Most issues trace to one of these four.
</Callout>

## If a specific email landed in spam

1. Check the subject line against the spam trigger word list: [Subject line best practices](/help/subject-line-best-practices)
2. Check the body for links: count them. More than 2 is risky.
3. Check if the recipient's domain has known strict filters (financial, healthcare, government)
4. If your custom domain is new: the domain reputation is still being established

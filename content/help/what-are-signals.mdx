# What are Signals?

Signals are optional, custom intent detectors. They watch for specific events that suggest a prospect is more likely to buy right now, then trigger a draft at that moment.

This article explains what Signals are, what they can detect, and when using them (or skipping them) makes sense.

## The core idea

Timing matters in cold email. Reaching a VP of Sales the week they post a LinkedIn message about needing more pipeline is very different from reaching them on a random Tuesday.

Signals let you define the exact event that matters for your ICP, then automate the response. When the event fires, Mirvo drafts a personalized email referencing that context.

## Signals are optional

<Callout type="info">
Without any Signals, Mirvo drafts emails for every prospect in your campaign. Signals add a precision layer on top, they don't replace the base workflow. Most users run their first campaigns without Signals, then add them after learning what triggers replies.
</Callout>

If you have a target list and want to reach everyone on it, you don't need a Signal. Launch the campaign, approve the drafts, done.

Signals are the right tool when you want to reach prospects only at the right moment, and you know what that moment looks like.

## What Signals can detect

| Signal type | Example |
|---|---|
| Hiring | A target company posts a job for "SDR" or "Head of Sales" |
| Funding | A prospect raises a Series A or Series B |
| Competitor mention | A prospect posts about a competitor product or switching tools |
| Tech stack change | A prospect's company adopts a new tool in your category |
| Leadership change | A new VP of Sales joins a target account |
| Product launch | A prospect launches a new product or enters a new market |
| Web mention | A prospect mentions a specific pain point publicly |
| News coverage | A prospect is featured in press around a topic you address |

You can combine conditions (hiring SDR AND Series A raised) for higher precision.

## How Signals integrate with a campaign

When you add a Signal to a campaign:

1. Mirvo scans for matching events every night
2. When a match fires on a prospect already in your campaign's scope, a draft is queued
3. The draft references the signal context in the email body
4. It lands in your Approval Queue like any other draft

Prospects in your campaign who never trigger the Signal don't get a draft from it. That's by design: the Signal is a qualifier, not a replacement for your prospect list.

## When to use Signals

Use Signals when:

- You have a specific buying trigger you know predicts purchase intent
- You want to reach a prospect at a specific moment rather than at any time
- You're targeting high-value accounts where timing matters more than volume

## When to skip Signals

Skip Signals when:

- You're just getting started and want to learn what messaging resonates first
- Your buyers don't have obvious external buying signals
- Your campaign is time-sensitive and you need to reach everyone now

You can always add a Signal to an existing campaign later.

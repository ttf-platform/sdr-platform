# Subject line best practices

Your subject line determines whether the email gets opened. This article covers what works for cold outbound and what gets you filtered.

## The goal: get opened, not go viral

Cold outbound subject lines don't need to be clever. They need to not look like marketing. The inbox is noise and your subject line has one job: look like it belongs there.

The reference point is a subject line you'd write to a colleague. Short, specific, no punctuation theater.

## What works

**Short and direct.** 3-7 words. No fluff.

Good examples:
- `scaling your outbound?`
- `quick question about Acme's hiring`
- `intro from Sarah`
- `your Series B timing`

**Lowercase or sentence case.** Title Case Subject Lines Look Like Newsletters. Lowercase or sentence case looks like a person wrote it.

**References something specific.** A company name, a recent event, a role. Not a category ("B2B founders") but a concrete detail ("your recent funding").

**Ends with a question mark sometimes.** Questions read as personal. Not every subject line needs one, but 1 in 3 is a reasonable ratio.

## What to avoid

**Spam trigger words.** Subject lines with these words increase the chance of going to spam:
- `free`, `guaranteed`, `no risk`
- `limited time`, `urgent`, `act now`
- `increase your sales`, `grow your revenue`
- `congratulations`, `winner`
- Excessive punctuation: `!!!`, `???`

**All caps.** EVEN ONE WORD.

**Vague teases.** "You won't believe this" or "Quick question" with no context works less and less. Overused to the point of recognition.

**Emoji.** Not universal. Filtered more aggressively by corporate email servers.

**Misleading re: / fwd: prefixes.** Some senders fake reply threads. This destroys trust and gets accounts flagged.

## Subject line and first sentence work together

The inbox preview shows your subject + the first 50-80 characters of the body. Think of them as a two-line unit.

Subject: `scaling your outbound?`
Preview: `I saw Acme added 3 SDR roles this month — usually a signal that...`

That two-line combination reads as a real email, not a sequence blast.

<Callout type="tip">
Write the first sentence of the email before finalizing the subject line. Read them as a pair. If the preview text starts with "Hi [Name], I hope this finds you well" — rewrite both.
</Callout>

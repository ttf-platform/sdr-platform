# Auto-scan and Approval Queue

When a Signal fires, Mirvo moves from detection to draft automatically. This article explains the full pipeline: from scan to draft to queue.

## The pipeline

```
Nightly scan → Signal match → Draft generated → Approval Queue
```

Each step happens without you doing anything. You only act at the last step: approving or rejecting the draft.

## When scans run

Signals scan nightly, typically between 2am-6am UTC. Each scan processes all active Signals in your workspace.

The scan cadence means there's a natural lag. If a prospect posts something at 3pm, it won't surface until the next morning's scan.

## What triggers a draft

A match is created when:

1. A prospect in a campaign scope matches the Signal condition
2. The same prospect hasn't already been matched by this Signal in the last 30 days (deduplication)
3. The prospect hasn't already been sent through the email sequence

All three conditions must be true. If a prospect already has an active sequence, the Signal match is logged but no draft is generated.

## Draft generation

After a match, the AI generates a draft using:

- The prospect's data
- Your Master ICP
- The matched signal event (what happened, when, why it was flagged)

The signal context is included in the draft to make the first sentence grounded in a real, recent event.

<Callout type="tip">
In the Approval Queue, expand a draft to see the Signal context panel. It shows the matched event that triggered the draft. Check it before approving. If the match looks off, reject and the signal deduplication window prevents a re-match for 30 days.
</Callout>

## Appearance in the queue

Signal-triggered drafts appear in the Approval Queue like any other draft, with one addition: a Signal badge showing which Signal triggered the draft.

You can filter the queue to show only signal-triggered drafts:

**Approval Queue, Filters, Signal: [your signal name]**

## Volume and pacing

If a signal fires for 50 prospects in one scan, 50 drafts arrive in the queue the same morning. This is expected behavior, not a bug.

If that volume is too high, tighten your Signal conditions (narrower keywords, role filter, company stage) to reduce the match rate. You can also pause a campaign temporarily to stop draft generation.

## Campaigns without signals

Campaigns without Signals generate drafts for all prospects immediately after launch. Drafts appear in the queue within minutes to an hour depending on queue depth.

Campaigns with Signals only generate drafts when a match occurs. Prospects can sit in scope for weeks without a draft if no signal fires for them.

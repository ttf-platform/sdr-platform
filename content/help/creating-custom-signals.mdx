# Creating custom signals

This article covers building a Signal from scratch. If you want to understand what Signals are first, start with [What are Signals?](/help/what-are-signals).

## When to create a custom Signal

The built-in Signals (funding, hiring, news) cover the most common triggers. Create a custom Signal when:

- You're watching for something specific to your ICP (e.g., "VP of Sales at a company using Salesforce who just posted about pipeline problems")
- You want to combine multiple conditions (e.g., hiring AND recent funding)
- You're targeting a narrow niche where the built-in signals produce too much noise

## Step 1. Open the Signal builder

Go to **Signals** in the sidebar, then click **New Signal**.

<Screenshot src='/help/screenshots/creating-custom-signals-1.png' alt='Signals list page with "New Signal" button highlighted' caption='Signals list page with "New Signal" button highlighted' />

## Step 2. Name your Signal

Names are internal only. Be specific: "Series A + hiring SDRs" is more useful than "growth signal."

## Step 3. Choose a signal source

Sources determine what data the Signal scans:

| Source | What it scans |
|---|---|
| Web activity | Company news, press mentions, blog posts |
| Job postings | Open roles by title, keyword, department |
| Funding events | New rounds by stage, size, or investor |
| LinkedIn activity | Posts, job changes (requires LinkedIn connection) |

Select one or more sources. Each source adds to the potential match surface.

<Callout type="info">
Source availability depends on your plan. Check Settings, Billing to see which sources are active for your workspace.
</Callout>

## Step 4. Set conditions

Conditions filter the raw signal events down to the ones you care about.

**Keyword conditions:** The signal fires when a scanned document contains these terms. Separate terms with commas for OR logic. Use "must contain all" toggle for AND logic.

Examples:
- `sales enablement, revenue operations` (fires if either appears)
- Must contain all: `CRM` + `implementation` (fires only when both appear)

**Role filter:** Narrow by the prospect's title. The signal only fires for prospects whose role matches.

**Company conditions:** Filter by company size, stage, or industry. Useful when you want the trigger to be relevant only for a specific company profile.

<Screenshot placeholder alt='Signal condition builder showing keyword field, role filter, and company conditions panel' caption='Signal condition builder showing keyword field, role filter, and company conditions panel' />

## Step 5. Preview matches

Before saving, click **Preview matches**. This shows you recent events that would have matched your conditions, pulled from the last 30 days of data.

Check:
- Are these the kind of companies you actually want to reach?
- Are there false positives? (matches that look right but aren't)

If the preview is empty, your conditions may be too narrow. If it's full of irrelevant results, tighten the keyword or add a role filter.

## Step 6. Save and attach

Save the Signal. Then attach it to a campaign in the campaign wizard under "Buying signal targeting."

<Callout type="tip">
A Signal you've never attached to a campaign still runs its scans nightly. It just doesn't produce drafts. You can attach it later and the matching history is preserved.
</Callout>

## Editing a Signal later

Go to **Signals, [your signal], Edit conditions** to modify conditions at any time. Changes take effect on the next nightly scan.

Signal edits don't retroactively change existing drafts in the Approval Queue.
